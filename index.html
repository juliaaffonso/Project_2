Human Error Behind Most Highway Accidents in Brazil

Data classified with the help of Artificial Intelligence shows that drivers' delayed or inefficient reactions, alcohol or drug use, and incompatible speed are among the causes of crashes

By Julia Affonso

The vast majority of traffic accidents on highways in Brazil, between 2021 and May 2025, were caused by human error. The information was obtained after nearly 300,000 accidents were classified with the help of Artificial Intelligence.

Over the last 4 years, driver errors were responsible for 81% of the accidents. Causes include drivers’ delayed or inefficient reactions, alcohol or drug consumption, incompatible speed, falling asleep at the wheel, or using a cellphone while driving.

Another 13% occurred due to factors beyond the drivers’ control. These include, for example: fog, rain, potholes, poor lighting, accidents during robberies, and malfunctioning traffic lights.

A smaller share of crashes involved other causes, such as brake problems or sharp curves. These were placed in a third category, in which it was not possible to state with confidence whether the crash was caused by human error.

The data also show that 79% of the accidents resulted in at least one person injured. The analysis considers crashes that occurred on highways and does not include accidents within cities' urban areas.

Also, between the beginning of 2021 and the end of 2024, there was a concentration of collisions in the weeks leading up to Christmas and New Year’s. The year 2025 was excluded from this specific analysis, as data were only available through May — the current year is not yet complete.

The number of accidents caused by human error rose by one percentage point between 2023 and 2024. In absolute numbers, however, this represented 5,089 more crashes on the roads.

When alcohol and drugs play a role

Brazilian law enforces zero tolerance for alcohol in traffic. Driving under the influence of alcoholic beverages or any other psychoactive substance is a very serious offense.

Of the 298,693 traffic accidents recorded by the Brazilian Federal Highway Police on federal roads, 6% were caused by alcohol and drug use -- the majority on weekends.

The state of Santa Catarina, in the south of the country, accounts for the majority of these cases. Since 2021, 2,847 accidents linked to alcohol or drugs have been recorded.

This analysis has found that 72% of the crashes occurred within a 5-kilometer (3.1-mile) radius of a bar, restaurant, or nightclub.

Another 720 crashes (28%) occurred outside this perimeter.

The average distance between an accident and nightlife venues is 3.5 kilometers (2.1 miles).

Bars, restaurants, and nightclubs were mapped in Santa Catarina with OpenStreetMap — a free, open map updated by volunteers.

The largest concentration of establishments is found near cities with more than 200,000 inhabitants, such as Florianópolis and eight others.

The distance between a crash and a bar, restaurant, or nightclub does not imply a correlation. It simply identifies the location of the crash and the presence of that type of establishment.

Santa Catarina is the sixth state with the highest number of registered vehicles in Brazil. Among them, it has the highest number of automobiles per capita.

Methodology

Data used: Brazilian Federal Highway Police data from 2021 to May 2025.

With the help of ChatGPT, accident causes were categorized into: human error, not human error, and other (when the cause could not be confidently determined).

Maps were created using QGIS and OpenStreetMap.

Charts were produced using DataWrapper and ObservableHQ.
